Engineering Specification Document

Document ID: ENG-SPEC-2023-001

Revision: 1.0

Date: October 15, 2023

1.0 Introduction

This document outlines the technical specifications for the new generation of industrial sensors. The sensors are designed to operate in harsh environments with temperatures ranging from -40°C to 85°C.

2.0 Technical Requirements

2.1 Operating Parameters

  • Voltage Range: 3.3V ±5%
  • Current Consumption: < 15mA in active mode
  • Communication: I2C and SPI interfaces

2.2 Environmental Specifications

  • Operating Temperature: -40°C to 85°C
  • Storage Temperature: -55°C to 125°C
  • Humidity: 0% to 95% non-condensing

3.0 Mechanical Dimensions

The sensor housing measures 25mm × 25mm × 5mm with mounting holes positioned at 20mm centers.

4.0 Calibration Procedure

Each unit must undergo a 3-point calibration at temperatures of -20°C, 25°C, and 70°C. Calibration data is stored in non-volatile memory.

Engineering Specification Document

Document ID: ENG-SPEC-2023-001

Revision: 2.0

Date: November 5, 2023

1.0 Introduction

This document outlines the technical specifications for the new generation of industrial sensors. The sensors are designed to operate in harsh environments with temperatures ranging from -45°C to 90°C.

2.0 Technical Requirements

2.1 Operating Parameters

  • Voltage Range: 3.3V ±10%
  • Current Consumption: < 12mA in active mode
  • Communication: I2C and SPI interfaces
  • Additional UART interface for legacy systems

2.2 Environmental Specifications

  • Operating Temperature: -45°C to 90°C
  • Storage Temperature: -55°C to 125°C
  • Humidity: 0% to 95% non-condensing
  • IP67 rating for dust and water resistance

3.0 Mechanical Dimensions

The sensor housing measures 24mm × 24mm × 4.5mm with mounting holes positioned at 20mm centers.

4.0 Calibration Procedure

Each unit must undergo a 5-point calibration at temperatures of -20°C, 25°C, and 70°C-30°C, 0°C, 25°C, 50°C, and 80°C. Calibration data is stored in non-volatile memory.

5.0 Quality Assurance

All units must pass a 48-hour burn-in test at 85°C before shipment. Failure rate must not exceed 0.1%.
